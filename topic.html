What's Adaptive Learning All About?

In the fields of education and learning, there's a growing buzz around adaptive learning technology. Conceptualized by B. F. Skinner in the 1950s, this idea started with a teaching machine that adapted questions based on previous answers, allowing students to learn at their own pace. Fast forward to today, and we've got AI supercharging this concept (Weir, 2019). (Weir, 2019)

Since the 1950s, tech has evolved like crazy. One big leap is in artificial intelligence (AI). The “AI boom” in the 1970s and 1980s showed us just how powerful machines can be at processing data and learning from it. Thanks to this, we now have advanced adaptive learning systems that use AI and machine learning to create a dynamic and responsive learning environment (Nawaf Alajlani, 2023).

Adaptive learning systems tweak the content, pace, and instructions in real-time based on a student's needs. By analyzing personal data, these systems offer personalized education, boosting engagement and promoting self-driven problem-solving skills (Alam, 2023). As AI continues to evolve, these systems will keep getting better, ensuring top-notch personalized learning experiences.

Topic Justification

The Pandemic Effect

In 2020, the global pandemic flipped the education world upside down, pushing everyone into online learning overnight (Junio & Bandala, 2023). This sudden shift turbocharged the adoption of digital tech in schools. With everyone scrambling to adapt, the spotlight turned to adaptive learning systems powered by AI and machine learning. These systems offered a way to keep education personal and effective, even from a distance (Nawaf Alajlani, 2023). Exploring these systems is not just fascinating but essential to understanding their vast potential and inherent risks.

Long-Term Implications

The importance of adaptive learning systems extends far beyond the immediate needs of online learning in today’s learning culture. These systems remain important as they have the potential to address long-term educational challenges, which traditional teaching can not support. By leveraging adaptive learning systems and data the systems can “continuously adjust” (Slomp, Ropelato, Bonatti, & Silva, 2024) and create a personalised learning experience and optimise efficiency of how they gain their information. With the use of AI, all types of learning can be tailored to, as “differentiation” is fundamental principle of adaptive learning systems (Slomp, Ropelato, Bonatti, & Silva, 2024).

A Tool for Teachers

Additionally, the implications of adaptive learning systems are important to teachers. As a data driven method of education, adaptive learning systems provide an accurate representation of a student’s progress and performance in relation to specific topics, questions, and other areas of their learning. With this data, teachers can maximise their effort and ensure that all students meet the requirements in the most appropriate and effective way possible.

Conclusion

Exploring adaptive learning systems is both timely and crucial in today’s educational climate. The advancements of AI and machine learning have presented opportunities to the fields of education and learning which can revolutionise the traditional methods, ensuring that all students are provided with the best education possible. However, the risks with this advancing technology must also be recognised and investigated to be managed currently.
